Why Bakersfield Homeowners Call A&M First

Bakersfield's unique geography and climate create specific septic challenges that national chains simply don't understand. The valley's clay-heavy soil — a defining characteristic of Kern County — doesn't drain the way sandy soils do. This means septic systems here fill faster, leach fields fail more frequently, and the average recommended pumping cycle is shorter than the national average.

Add Bakersfield's intense summer heat — routinely exceeding 105°F — and the bacterial load in septic systems accelerates dramatically. A system that could go 4 years between pumpings in San Francisco may need servicing every 2 years in Bakersfield.

Bakersfield Septic Regulations You Need to Know

The Kern County Environmental Health Services Division oversees septic system permits, inspections, and compliance in Bakersfield. Key requirements include:

  • Septic systems must be inspected before any property sale or major renovation in unincorporated Kern County
  • Grease interceptors in commercial food service establishments must be cleaned based on a frequency schedule determined by grease accumulation — typically every 30–90 days
  • All septic waste must be disposed of at a licensed receiving facility — A&M uses only Kern County approved disposal sites

Our team handles all required manifests and compliance documentation. We've helped dozens of Bakersfield property owners navigate the inspection and permit process when selling or upgrading their homes.
