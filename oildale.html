Septic Service Built for Oildale's Unique Needs

Oildale is one of the oldest unincorporated communities in Kern County, and its residential infrastructure reflects that history. Many homes in Oildale were built before modern septic codes were established, meaning older-style systems — some approaching 50+ years of service — are still in active use.

A&M Pumping specializes in servicing these older systems. We understand what to look for in aging cesspools and steel tanks, and we provide honest assessments about whether systems need repair, replacement, or just regular maintenance.

Common Septic Problems We See in Oildale

  • Aging steel tanks — corroding and collapsing in older Oildale homes built pre-1970
  • Undersized systems — many properties have tanks too small for current household sizes
  • Saturated leach fields — Oildale's dense clay soil creates high failure rates
  • Cesspool conversions — required when cesspools are discovered during property sales

How Often Should Oildale Homeowners Pump Their Septic Tank?

Given the age of many Oildale systems and the challenging clay soil conditions, we recommend annual inspections and pumping every 1.5 to 2.5 years for most households — more frequently than the Bakersfield average due to system age and soil type. Don't wait for problems; prevention is always cheaper than emergency repair.
